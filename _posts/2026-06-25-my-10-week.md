## SQL Joins: Merging tables using INNER JOIN, LEFT JOIN, and RIGHT JOIN

In relational databases, data is intentionally split across multiple tables to avoid redundancy. However, to extract meaningful insights, you must combine them using **SQL Joins**. Joins match rows from different tables based on a related column between them.



We primarily rely on three fundamental types of joins to cross-reference our engineering data:
1. **INNER JOIN:** Retrieves only the records that have matching values in both tables.
2. **LEFT JOIN:** Retrieves all records from the left table, along with the matching records from the right table (unmatched right rows return NULL).
3. **RIGHT JOIN:** Retrieves all records from the right table, along with the matching records from the left table (unmatched left rows return NULL).

```sql
-- Combining student records with their corresponding hostel room allocations
SELECT Students.FullName, Students.RollNumber, HostelRooms.RoomNumber
FROM Students
INNER JOIN HostelRooms 
ON Students.StudentID = HostelRooms.StudentID;
