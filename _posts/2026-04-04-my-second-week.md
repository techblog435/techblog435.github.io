## Linear Regression Foundations

When we look at the points scored throughout the tournament, we are looking at **continuous data**. To understand our "winning momentum," we can use **Linear Regression**.

At its core, Linear Regression is about finding the **"Line of Best Fit."** Imagine plotting every game's performance on a graph. The line of best fit is the path that stays as close as possible to all those data points simultaneously, minimizing the "error" or "noise."

$$y = mx + b$$

In this equation:
* **$y$**: Our predicted outcome (Total Points).
* **$x$**: The input (Number of Games played).
* **$m$ (The Slope)**: This represents our "Winning Rate." A steeper slope means we are gaining points faster than other teams.
* **$b$ (The Intercept)**: Where we started at the beginning of the gala.

## Predictive Trends: Forecasting the Future

By calculating the **Slope ($m$)** and the **Intercept ($b$)**, we can actually forecast future outcomes. If the Electrical Engineering department maintains this specific slope, we can predict exactly how many points we will have by the final championship game.

This is the power of engineering: we don't just celebrate the win; we calculate the trend, optimize the performance, and predict the next victory.
