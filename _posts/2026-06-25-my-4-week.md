Here are the critical visualization tools used to run diagnostics on raw data before model training:

## Heatmaps and Correlation: Finding Hidden Relationships
Before building predictive models, you need to understand how your variables interact. A correlation matrix calculated over your dataset reveals whether features move together, move in opposite directions, or have no relationship at all. Turning this matrix into a visual **Heatmap** uses color gradients to make strong dependencies immediately obvious. This helps identify multicollinearity—where two features provide identical information—allowing you to streamline your inputs before training.

## Box Plots: Identifying Distribution Anomalies
While summary statistics give you averages, they often mask the true shape of your data. A **Box Plot** splits your data into quartiles, highlighting the median, the interquartile range (IQR), and the minimum and maximum boundaries. Any data point that falls beyond the "whiskers" of the box plot is instantly flagged as a statistical outlier. Visually isolating these anomalies tells you exactly which records require cleaning or capping before they bias your machine learning algorithms.
